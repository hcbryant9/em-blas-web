Installation
Cyanotypes

Cyanotypes is a six channel sound collage consisting of five individual voices and their associated ramblings, each assigned to an individual speaker. The listener is encouraged to move around the space to listen to the individual speakers or simply sit and take in the stories as each voice slowly modulates louder and softer. Utilizing five voices and five silhouettes, this installation explores the tension between vulnerability and humor through the stories we tell.

Memory as a Mass

Memory as a Mass concerns the experience of life through sensory and emotionally charged events. Experiences of pleasure, pain, and the people met remain fresh in the memory while the rest is a wash of uncertainty. This installation includes a slide projector, a wooden table, 140 35mm found slides, a small area rug, a slide projection screen, and sun bleached red construction paper fastened to the windows with black duct tape. Found experiences seem almost dreamlike as the broken projector shuffles and advances to individual film slides at random. The warm pink light filtering through the construction paper is reminiscent of a bodily interior. The 1960s-1970s era images include family photos, domestic interiors, surgical documentation, faces, landscapes, and mistakenly overexposed photos. Family photos invoke feelings of happiness while the appearance of a surgery is a quick reminder of painful memories. Hazy, overexposed images suggest a feeling of confusion in the attempt to remember something you can’t. Uncomfortably close photographs of faces persuade the viewer to endure direct eye contact with them for twenty seconds, making one feel that they are observed, even from their own memory.
